A note about MLE

In this class, we have presented a general purpose strategy for estimating \(\hat{F}_X\) via MLE. For specific models, there may exist specific methods or algorithms.

For example

  • For the normal model, the MLE solution is

\[\hat{\mu} = \bar{X};\ \ \widehat{\sigma^2} = \frac{N-1}{N}s^2\approx s^2\]

(For large \(N\), the \(\frac{N-1}{N}\) term is often omitted.)

Compare bootstrap and simulation

  • Note the similarities.
  • Recall that the ECDF is an estimate of \(F_X\).
  • The bootstrap is sampling from the ECDF estimate of \(F_X\).
  • The second simulation is sampling from the method of moments estimate of \(F_X\).

A trick to speed-up simulation

Setting: Generating the sampling distribution of a quantile related summary measure (min, median, max, etc.)

Rather than simulating \(N \text{ (Sample size)} \times R \text{ (replicates)}\) draws of the data, we can take advantage of the properties of order statistics and only simulate \(R\) draws.

A trick to speed-up simulation

Setting: Generating sampling distribution of a quantile related summary measure (min, median, max, etc.)

The trick: \[\text{If } U \sim \text{uniform}(0,1),\text{ then }U_{(k)} \sim \text{beta}(k, N-k+1)\] In words: The order statistics distribution for uniform data is the beta distribution.

Example with Method of Moments

# Estimation outside loop
R <- 5000
out1 <- rep(NA, R)
scale_hat <- var(alb)/mean(alb)
shape_hat <- mean(alb)^2/var(alb)
for(i in 1:R){
  out1[i] <- rgamma(N_alb, shape = shape_hat, scale = scale_hat) %>% var
}

# Estimation inside loop
out2 <- rep(NA, R)
for(i in 1:R){
  boot_alb <- sample(alb, N_alb, replace = TRUE)
  scale_hat <- var(boot_alb)/mean(boot_alb)
  shape_hat <- mean(boot_alb)^2/var(boot_alb)
  out2[i] <- rgamma(N_alb, shape = shape_hat, scale = scale_hat) %>% var
}

boxplot(list(`Estimation outside loop` = out1, `Estimation inside loop` = out2))

Big picture

Middle 95% of the sampling distribution

The interval corresponding to the middle 95% of the sampling distribution is called the 95% Confidence Interval. It will play an important role when we discuss inference formally.

Middle 95% of the sampling distribution

The middle 95% of a distribution is not a precisely defined term.

Option 1: The interval that results by calculating the 0.025 and 0.975 quantiles. (This is sometimes called the symmetric density interval.)

Option 2: The shortest interval that contains 95% of the density. (This is called the highest density interval.)

Middle 95% of the sampling distribution

If the distribution is symmetric, then the symmetric density interval will be the same as the highest density interval.

The highest density interval is helpful if the distribution is not symmetric.

SDI vs HDI
